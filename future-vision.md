# Trust Assembly — Future Vision

*A roadmap for building the credibility infrastructure of the future.*

---

## A Note on Our Structure

Trust Assembly intends to register as a **nonprofit organization** before accepting any revenue. We will also accept **donations** from individuals and foundations who share this vision. Every dollar that enters the system will be used to support its growth, with a primary focus on rewarding participants for high quality work and to build a support staff and architecture.

---

## How Trust Assembly Will Make Money

The platform is free to use. The corrections are free to read. But the process of producing verified truth at scale requires real resources — jurors who are compensated for careful review, infrastructure that runs reliably, and development that keeps the system ahead of those who would game it.

**All money flows through to preserve credibility and fund it.** Nobody in the system is paid to reach a specific conclusion. The money is fuel. The credibility engine runs on the math. A republic-style voting process will be introduced in later iterations so that new rules can be added as needed to protect the integrity of the system.

We intend to pursue the following revenue streams, with a primary focus on **subscriptions** and **bounties**.

### Subscriptions

Anyone can follow any Assembly for free. Assemblies are lenses — curated perspectives from communities whose values and rigor you trust. Extension users will see a call to action to **support the work** of the Assemblies they follow by subscribing. Subscription fees flow directly to the Assembly's jury payment pool. An **Institutional tier** provides API access, bulk feeds, and analytics for organizations integrating Trust Assembly's credibility signals.

### Prominence & Visibility Rules

Rules will govern what content is elevated: high-quality work is pushed to prominence, low-quality work is filtered before it reaches the visibility layer. At scale, we will also provide **ratings for entire websites, organizations, and public figures**. Prominence rules will apply when these features are enabled.

> **The Illuminatus Uncle Rule** — you don't need a system this powerful because you want to win an argument with your uncle at Thanksgiving. Prominence exists to serve the public interest, not personal vendettas.

### Bounties

Anyone can post a bounty by submitting a **piece of media and related information**. Bounties must be a neutral set of facts in need of analysis — **no point of view permitted**. High-reputation submitters get first access. The cost includes funds for jurors, submitters, administration, and a **standing challenge** — a bet no one can overturn the ruling, which grows as disputes are filed. Dispute losers forfeit their matching stake: a portion goes to the review team, the rest increases the standing challenge. Trust Assembly generates revenue in part by holding bounty funds in escrow.

### Where Money Applies

**Disputes and cross-group reputation are the only places where money applies.** Inside an Assembly, all activity is free. Money to accelerate in-group review is voluntarily donated by subscribers.

### Appeal Adjudication

The **person requesting the appeal pays Trust Assembly**, which distributes the fee: jurors are compensated, a portion is returned to the originating platform, and Trust Assembly retains a small administration fee. The jury evaluates content against the platform's own policies.

### Review Verification

A **revenue source for participating Assemblies** — specialized review Assemblies earn fees for verification work. Ad revenue from a consumer-facing review portal provides additional revenue.

### Reputation Insurance

*Will activate when the system has sufficient scale to deliver on these promises.* Subscribers get priority access to expedited review when targeted by misinformation.

### The AI Credibility Vault

Trust Assembly intends to become a **fact vault for AI companies** — a verified dataset that can meaningfully influence and steer large language models as these technologies become more powerful.

## The Larger Vision

Everything described above is **one leg** of a larger system.

### An AI-Compatible Government — The Forum

Unlike the correction system — which measures accuracy — the Forum measures **desirability, fairness, and justice**. Is this policy fair? Does it serve the people it claims to serve? Citizens evaluate proposals on these dimensions.

### Political Accountability Tracking

Campaign commitments become verifiable claims measured by the same transparent system.

### Delegated Voting & Representative Authority

A **personalized voting guide on your phone** — choices curated by trusted members of your community, not a party or an algorithm.

### The Connective Tissue

The credibility bank doesn't just tell you who to trust about what happened yesterday. It tells you who to trust about what should happen tomorrow.
